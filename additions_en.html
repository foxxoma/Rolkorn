# DEPTH AND PROGRESSION

For groups that want more tactical depth and a sense of progression in a long-term campaign.

Skills and Proficiency

Your background is still important, but now your key abilities are outlined in a skills table. The description now provides a maximum bonus of +1, but retains the impact of weaknesses up to -4.


Creating Skills

Compile a list of skills that define your character.

A skill is a specific area in which you are strong: "Bladed Weapons", "Stealth", "Fire Magic", "Persuasion".

Each skill is assigned a Proficiency value — this is the bonus you add to rolls related to that skill.

During character creation:

  • Most of your skills have a Proficiency of 1.
  • One skill (your main one) has a Proficiency of 2.

Improving Skills

After successfully overcoming a serious challenge where a skill was critical, or after extensive training, the Game Master may allow you to increase that skill's Proficiency by 1.

Important: If you go a long time without using a skill (e.g., several sessions in a row without being in situations that require it), its Proficiency may decrease to 1. This reflects a loss of practice.


Limitations via Condition Tracks

Use Condition Tracks to manage special abilities and resources (mana, stamina, concentration).

Create tracks that suit your setting and character concept: "Mana", "Stamina", "Mental Fortitude".

Using special abilities may require spending points from these tracks. For example, casting a spell might require filling 1 box on the "Mana" track.

Costs are paid without a roll — you simply pay the price.

Also, the number of boxes on a track can increase when a related skill's Proficiency improves.


Creating Special Abilities

You can create unique abilities for your character.

  • Description: Describe what the ability does and how it works in the world.
  • Requirements: Set a minimum Proficiency value for the corresponding skill.
  • Cost: Specify how many boxes and from which Condition Tracks must be spent to use the ability.
Fireball
Description: You create and hurl a sphere of fire that damages enemies.
Requirements: Proficiency in the "Fire Magic" skill of at least 3.
Cost: 1 box on the "Mana" track.

How Skills Impact the Game

Skills and special abilities can influence the game in various ways:

  • Roll Bonuses: The primary use — adding your Proficiency to a d20 roll.
  • Narrative Component: A skill can grant you the right to an automatic success in simple situations or the ability to attempt things that would be impossible without the skill.
  • Rounds: Some abilities can increase or decrease the number of rounds in a Tense situation (for you or your opponent).
  • Opponent's Condition Tracks: Abilities can allow you to immediately fill boxes on an opponent's Condition Tracks (e.g., "Fear" or "Wounds").
  • ... In general, they can influence anything; the main thing is to balance them.

Equipment

Equipment can now provide permanent or situational bonuses.

  • Armor: Provides a bonus to defense (e.g., +1 to the difficulty for opponents attacking you).
  • Weapons: Provides a bonus to attack (e.g., +1 to attack rolls).
  • Artifacts: Can grant special abilities or skill bonuses.
Steel Sword
Grants +1 to attack rolls if you have the "Bladed Weapons" skill.
Cloak of Shadows
Once per scene, allows you to reroll a die on a Stealth check.

Important Note

These rules are tools. Use them as needed. Don't let them overshadow the story. The numbers are recommendations, but you can adjust them to suit your pace and style of play.
